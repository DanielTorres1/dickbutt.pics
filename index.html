About Dick Butt

Dick Butt is an illustration of an anthropomorphic phallus with a pair of testicles and a penis protruding from its backside. Originally created by webcomic artist K.C. Green in 2006, the character has become one of the internet's most recognizable and enduring memes.

The drawing is most commonly used in multi-pane image macros and animated GIFs where Dick Butt is revealed unexpectedly in the final frame, serving as a form of visual bait-and-switch or Rick Roll-style surprise. The character's absurd design and unexpected appearances have made it a beloved fixture of internet humor culture.

"Dick Butt represents the internet's love for the absurd, the unexpected, and the hilariously juvenile. It's art, but not as we know it." - Internet Historians, probably

Origin & History

July 2, 2006

The Birth of Dick Butt

Artist K.C. Green published a webcomic titled "Tree. You've Been Good to Us" as part of the Horribleville series. The comic featured an artist who illustrates the Dick Butt character after being scolded for wasting tree resources as a child.

This origin story gives Dick Butt a meta-textual quality - it's literally a character created as a form of artistic rebellion against waste, which ironically became one of the internet's most wastefully shared images.

The original Horribleville comic from July 2nd, 2006

August 2007

4chan Emergence

On August 27th, 2007, 4chan user VIPPER spammed the phrase "DICK BUTT" in a thread on the 4chan BBS, marking one of the earliest coordinated efforts to spread the meme beyond its original comic context.

June 2009

Batman Multi-Pane Comic

BuzzFeed highlighted a multi-pane machine code comic in which Dick Butt is revealed in a note from the superhero Batman, demonstrating the meme's evolution into the "bait-and-switch" format that would become its signature style.

Multi-pane Batman comic featuring Dick Butt

July 2010

DeviantArt & Fan Art Explosion

DeviantArtist beyx uploaded an illustration of a Dick Butt character with large eyes sitting on a rainbow, marking the beginning of widespread fan art interpretations. On October 30th, 2011, the Xkcdickbutt Tumblr blog was launched, highlighting Dick Butt comics illustrated in the style of the Xkcd series.

DeviantArt reimagining with rainbow (2010)

April 2012

4chan Integration

FunnyJunk user atlantaboy submitted a screenshot of Dick Butt used in a 4chan thread, showcasing how the meme had become fully integrated into imageboard culture as a standard trolling and surprise element.

Dick Butt used in 4chan threads (2012)

December 2013

College Humor Recognition

Internet humor site College Humor posted a compilation of notable Dick Butt animated GIFs, signaling the meme's transition from underground internet culture to mainstream recognition.

January 2014

Reddit Viral Moment

Redditor spacecadet06 submitted a photoshopped GIF featuring a scene from the Russian television show Matchmakers 6 in which the outline of Dick Butt emerges from a student's wood shaving art piece. The post gained over 30,800 upvotes and 670 comments in the /r/gifs subreddit within two months, becoming one of the most viral Dick Butt moments.

Viral wood carving GIF from Russian TV show (January 2014)

That same day, the /r/DickButt subreddit reached its first 100 subscribers milestone after receiving a record number of sign-ups since its creation in August 2008.

On January 16th, Redditor bonebowl submitted an animated GIF of a cyclist launching off a ramp onto an illustration of Dick Butt, garnering upwards of 11,900 upvotes and 190 comments in the following month.

Cyclist ramp GIF (January 2014)

2015-Present

Legendary Status & Cultural Icon

Dick Butt achieved legendary meme status, appearing in video games (as Easter eggs), merchandise, and even tattoos. K.C. Green embraced the meme's success while continuing his artistic work, occasionally revisiting the character.

The meme became a cultural icon of internet humor, representing a specific era of web culture characterized by absurdism, surprise reveals, and community-driven comedy.

About the Creator: K.C. Green

K.C. Green is an American webcomic artist best known for creating Dick Butt and the "This is Fine" dog meme (from his Gunshow comic series). His work is characterized by dark humor, absurdist scenarios, and expressive character designs.

Notable Works:

  • Horribleville - The series where Dick Butt originated
  • Gunshow - Creator of the "This is Fine" meme
  • Back - Ongoing fantasy adventure webcomic
  • Regular Show - Contributed to the Cartoon Network series

Despite creating two of the internet's most famous memes, K.C. Green continues to produce original content and has expressed mixed feelings about being primarily known for memes rather than his broader artistic work.

Legacy & Importance

Dick Butt represents a particular era and style of internet humor - one characterized by absurdism, unexpected reveals, and a rejection of conventional taste. Unlike many memes that rise and fall in popularity, Dick Butt has maintained consistent relevance for over 15 years.

The meme exemplifies how simple, crude drawings can achieve cultural significance through the internet's community-driven humor ecosystem. It's a testament to K.C. Green's artistic vision and the internet's ability to elevate the absurd to the iconic.

"Dick Butt is proof that you don't need complexity to create something memorable. Sometimes, the simplest, most ridiculous idea is what sticks with people forever."

Cultural Significance:

  • One of the most enduring memes of the 2000s-2010s period
  • Demonstrates the power of simple, memorable design in meme culture
  • Represents the internet's love for subversive, juvenile humor
  • Shows how independent artists can create globally recognized cultural artifacts
  • Part of the canon of "classic" internet memes alongside Pepe, Trollface, and Rage Comics

The Enduring Appeal

In a digital landscape constantly churning out new memes, Dick Butt remains a nostalgic favorite and an active part of internet culture. Its simplicity, absurdity, and perfect execution as a visual punchline ensure that it will continue appearing unexpectedly across the internet for years to come.
